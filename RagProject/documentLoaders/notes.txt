MUSCLE HYPERTROPHY - STRUCTURED NOTES

====================================
1) WHAT IS MUSCLE HYPERTROPHY?
====================================

Muscle hypertrophy means an increase in the size of muscle fibers.
It usually happens through resistance training + good nutrition + recovery.
The body adapts to repeated training stress by growing stronger and larger.

Key condition:
- Muscle protein synthesis must stay higher than muscle protein breakdown over time.


====================================
2) TYPES OF HYPERTROPHY
====================================

A) Myofibrillar hypertrophy
- Increases contractile proteins in muscle fibers.
- Improves strength and size.

B) Sarcoplasmic hypertrophy
- Increases fluid and energy components inside muscle cells.
- Improves muscular endurance and "full" muscle appearance.

Note:
- Both happen together, but training style may emphasize one more.


====================================
3) CORE PRINCIPLES OF MUSCLE GROWTH
====================================

A) Progressive Overload
- Gradually increase training challenge.
- Increase weight, reps, sets, control, or reduce rest.

B) Mechanical Tension
- Force produced by muscles under load.
- Best achieved with controlled form and challenging resistance.

C) Muscle Damage
- Micro-tears from hard training trigger repair and adaptation.

D) Metabolic Stress
- "Pump" effect from lactate/metabolite buildup.
- Common with higher reps and shorter rest.


====================================
4) TRAINING VARIABLES
====================================

A) Volume
- Usually measured as sets x reps x load.
- General target: 10-20 sets per muscle group per week.

B) Frequency
- Train each muscle group at least 2 times per week.
- Better volume distribution and recovery.

C) Intensity
- Common hypertrophy range: ~60%-85% of 1RM.

D) Repetition Range
- Typical: 6-12 reps.
- Effective growth can happen across a wider rep range if effort is high.

E) Rest Intervals
- Short rest (30-90 sec): more metabolic stress.
- Longer rest (2-3 min): better performance for heavy sets.


====================================
5) EXERCISE SELECTION
====================================

Compound movements (high overall mass potential):
- Squat
- Deadlift
- Bench press
- Row

Isolation movements (detail and symmetry):
- Bicep curl
- Tricep extension
- Lateral raise


====================================
6) TECHNIQUE AND EXECUTION
====================================

- Use full, safe range of motion.
- Keep controlled tempo, especially eccentric (lowering phase).
- Build mind-muscle connection for better muscle activation.


====================================
7) NUTRITION FOR HYPERTROPHY
====================================

- Maintain a small caloric surplus (~200-500 kcal/day).
- Protein target: ~1.6-2.2 g per kg body weight.
- Carbs fuel training sessions.
- Healthy fats support hormone function.


====================================
8) RECOVERY REQUIREMENTS
====================================

- Muscle grows during recovery, not during the workout itself.
- Sleep target: 7-9 hours per night.
- Poor recovery increases injury risk and stalls progress.


====================================
9) TRAINING SPLITS
====================================

A) Full Body Split
- Best for beginners.
- Usually 3 sessions per week.

B) Upper / Lower Split
- Great for intermediate lifters.
- Usually 4 sessions per week.

C) Push / Pull / Legs (PPL)
- Very popular for hypertrophy.
- Can be 3-day or 6-day schedule.

D) Bro Split
- One muscle group per day/week.
- Can work, but lower frequency may limit natural lifter progress.

E) Hybrid Split
- Customized combination to target weak points.


====================================
10) PRACTICAL SUCCESS CHECKLIST
====================================

- Train each muscle twice weekly minimum.
- Track load, reps, and sets.
- Maintain technique quality.
- Progress overload gradually.
- Eat enough calories and protein.
- Sleep consistently.
- Include a deload week every 6-8 weeks if needed.


====================================
11) CONCLUSION
====================================

Muscle hypertrophy is the result of smart training, proper nutrition,
and reliable recovery habits over time.

Consistency beats intensity spikes.
Sustainable progress creates long-term growth.
